In the first graph we answer the questions: What are the least developed countries? What qualifies the least developed countries and what regions are they located in? Through the graph we can see least developed countries locations and their corresponding regions indicated by color. Read the caption to see how UNICEF classifies the least developed countries. We see the least developed countries are concentrated in Africa and Asia, mostly Eastern and South Africa and West and Central Africa by region. From our previous knowledge about the developing world this was expected.

In the second graph we answer the question: What are the school completion rates in these countries? Which regions have the highest/lowest school completion rates? What is the average school completion rate in these countries? To answer these questions we created a barchart that length encodes the school completion rate in each country. Each bar is then color-coded based on the country’s region and then sorted so we can see the statistics compared to the rest of the countries in that region. We have also included annotations to show the lowest school completion rate belonging to the country of South Sudan located in the region of Eastern and Southern Africa. The highest school completion rate out of the least developed countries is Sao Tome and Principe located in the region of West and Central Africa. This was interesting to us since most of the least developed countries are concentrated in these two regions I would not have guessed the highest school completion rate would be in this area. We would’ve assumed it would be in an area more surrounded by not developing countries. However, on inspection of the map I saw that Sao Tome and Principe is an island off the west coast of Africa. This made us think that maybe the isolation of an island keeps Sao Tome and Principe away from the conflict and negative environment in Central Africa that could affect school completion. Regional averages are also shown with the lowest regional average in West and Central Africa and the highest in East Asia and Pacific.

In what way is wealth class and school completion rate correlated?
To find a pattern between class and completion rate, a line graph was drawn to detect any possible correlation. Instead of using time as the x-axis variable, I used the five different wealth classes. Even before visualizing this data, it is obvious that the higher the wealth class, the higher the school completion rate. The next question is to ask, how wide is the discrepancy between wealth classes? A line graph visualization was chosen because the slope is an easy visualization to observe. A wider discrepancy is indicated by a drastic slope in the line graph. Flatter lines indicate a narrower discrepancy between completion rates and wealth class. From the visualization, it seemed that the slopes were similar across different countries, indicating that the completion rate discrepancies were also similar. Another comparison could be made between the different countries. For example, although there are discrepancies, there is also a general difference between the countries of all classes. Some countries’ poor classes have higher school completion rates than the richest class of another country. For example, South Sudan’s richest class have a school completion rate of about 45%, while the poorest class of neighboring Sudan have about 49% school completion rate. The range of completion rates can differ drastically depending on the country.
In which countries does the poorest class have the highest school rate?
Because most people are likely to belong in the poorest class, they are also in most need of education. How does the poorest class between different countries compare in school completion rate? For this, a bar graph was created to compare the completion rates in the poorest class. The bars were color coded to indicate which subregion the country is in. The next question that came from this bar graph is whether there is a correlation between the school completion rate and the geography. By clicking on a specific region, the bars within that region will be highlighted. It can be seen that some regions do generally better than other regions. For example, South Asia has higher completion rates than does Eastern and Southern Africa. This raises another question; do countries near each other experience around the same level of the completion rates?
